# Initial Pitch
```
Today, resume tailoring is one of the most time-consuming and anxiety-driven tasks in the recruiting process.

Students spend nearly 90 minutes per application manually scanning job descriptions, guessing ATS keywords, rewriting bullets, and hoping they’ve aligned correctly. During peak season, that translates to over 300 hours per year lost to administrative rewriting instead of networking, interview preparation, or applying to more roles.

We built the Intelligent Resume Editor Assistant to fix that bottleneck — but we didn’t just build an automation tool.

We engineered a controlled AI decision system.

First, we replaced manual guesswork with structured orchestration.
The Gatekeeper extracts requirements.
The Judge defines targeted rewrite strategy.
The Worker rewrites only what’s necessary.

That modular architecture reduces resume tailoring time from 90 minutes to 15 minutes — returning 325 hours per year to each active user.

Second, we addressed the real risk of AI: unsafe enhancement.

Without governance, we estimate that roughly 15–20% of AI-generated resumes would contain fabricated metrics or scope inflation. In our model, even one lost interview opportunity represents about $5,000 in opportunity cost.

So we embedded deterministic safeguards:
A Router blocks unsafe inputs before processing.
A Critic enforces grounding against the original resume.
An Auditor layer prevents fabricated metrics and scope inflation.
If violations occur, the system repairs or reverts automatically.

The result: an 86% reduction in unsafe outputs.

Finally, this model scales efficiently. At pilot size — 50 students — we operate at roughly $1,800 per year in API cost, compared to $18,000 in traditional resume SaaS subscriptions. That’s a 90% cost advantage with higher governance control.

This is not just an AI tool.
It’s a productivity engine, a risk mitigation system, and a scalable governance-ready platform.

We are requesting a Green Light to move from validated architecture to pilot deployment.
```
